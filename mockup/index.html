1180 BC

The President


Age: 3200 yrs
Species: Giant Sequoia
Location: Visalia, California


Named after U.S. president Warren G. Harding in 1923, this giant sequoia is also the third largest tree in the world at about 45,000 cubic feet in diameter and 247 feet tall, containing nearly two billion leaves and more than eighty thousand cones. Its sap contains tannic acid which provides significant protection against the ravages of California's wildfires.

At around the time the Egyptians were inventing the sundial, a single Patagonian Cypress emerged from the rainforest floor in Southern Chile. 3622 years later, Gran Abuelo towers over its neighbors standing at 196 feet. It grows extremely slowly, gaining only one milimeter in diameter every year. Its inner bark contains special resins that helps the tree stave off decomposition even when buried or resting in water.

Sarv-e Abarkuh


Age: 4500 yrs
Species: Mediterranean cypress
Location: Abarkuh, Yzad, Iran


Likely to be the oldest living lifeform in Asia, legend goes that the tree was planted by Noah after the Great storm, while others claim that it was by the ancient Iranian prophet, Zoroaster. Standing 28 meters high and 11.5 meters wide in diameter, the tree is an evergreen that blooms every spring. It can be seen depicted in various art forms as far back as the walls of ancient persepolis.

Methuselah


Age: 4860 yrs
Species: Bristlecone Pine
Location: Inyo County, California


Still a seedling when King Tut was a boy, Methuselah still thrives today deep in the White Mountains along the California-Nevada border. To protect the tree, its exact location remains undisclosed and there are no confirmed photos in circulation. With the area receiving severe winds and less than 12" of rain per year, the tree has adapted by growing extremely slowly -no more than an inch per century.

Sisters Olive Trees of Noah


Age: 5000-6000 yrs
Species: European Olive
Location: Bcheale, Lebanon

The sisters consist of a grove of 16 olive trees, considered by locals to be over 6000 years old. Legend goes that The Sisters produced the single olive branch returned to Noah by a dove after the Great Flood. The trees produce olives to this day and the revenue from its oils continue to fund their conservation. The oil has notes of pepper, herbs de provence and almonds.
